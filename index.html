TECHNOLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS

Industry growth for HDTV has been slow, with only about 6 million U.S. house- holds owning high-definition television sets.

The primary inhibitor to growth has been the high cost of the television sets, which ranges from $1,000 to as much as $7,000.

Adding to the problem, less than one-third of all cable networks carry any high-definition programming.

Consumers generally have been unwilling to spend a significant amount of money for new HDTV sets when there is little programming to justify their expenditures.

Likewise, networks and program producers have been reluctant to incur the increased costs of high-definition broadcasting when only a small percentage of households have HDTVs.
